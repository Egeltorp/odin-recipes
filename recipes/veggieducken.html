Thanksgiving Special - The Veggie Turducken

By Henry Phillips on Henry's Kitchen

Link to source video here. Highly recommended watch!

Description

Today we're gonna be making Henry's f***ing Veggie Turducken, or as it is commonly known as the Veggieducken. The Veggieducken was first conceived by Dan Pashman in late 2012, but it is still served today at Thanksgiving feasts all over Los Angeles.

Pro tip: Most vegetarians are very friendly, although you wanna be careful not to give off a sense of being threatening or taunting in any way, because they could go into what's called a fight or flight response mode which could trigger them into aggressive activity. But buy and large you will find them to be a very friendly group.

Ingredients

  • 1 Giant Banana Squash
  • 2 Medium Yams
  • 3 Medium Leeks
  • 1/2 cup Olive Oil
  • 1/2 cup Vegetable Broth
  • 1 Yellow Onion
  • 6 Garlic Cloves
  • 4 cups Bread Crumbs
  • Parsley/Sage (for stuffing)
  • Bell Pepper?

Steps

  1. Place Food (onions, garlic) in Processor
  2. Add Bell Pepper
  3. Add it to our bowl
  4. We're gonna pour all that into a saute pan
  5. Saute in oil for about 5 minutes
  6. Socialize with guest(s) in the meantime
  7. 2 cups fresh parsley in food processor
  8. Grind it up!
  9. Start putting everything into work Bowl (parsley + onion mixture)
  10. Add to 1/4 cup olive oil, 4 cups bread crumps, to work bowl
  11. Stir it up!
  12. Cut Banana Squash in half (don't drop it!)
  13. Discard inside shit/stuff and put it into a bowl
  14. Add layer (of stuffing) to bottom (about a half inch thick)
  15. Lay in our leeks
  16. Add Sweet Potatoes
  17. Add yet another layer of stuffing
  18. Top it off with the other half of the Banana Squash
  19. Top it off!
  20. Bake overnight at 350°F
  21. Enjoy with a friend!
